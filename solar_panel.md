# Solar Panel

For an off-grid, solar-powered LoRa bridge in Portugal, optimizing your solar panel placement is critical to keeping those 21700 batteries charged year-round—especially through the shorter winter days.

Because Portugal sits in the Northern Hemisphere (roughly between 37ºN and 42ºN latitude), here is exactly how to position your NIVIAN 9W panel for peak efficiency.

## 1. Direction (Azimuth)

The Rule: Face the panel True South (180º azimuth).

Why: This ensures the panel catches the maximum amount of solar radiation as the sun tracks across the sky from east to west.

Peer Tip: Use a compass app on your phone, but remember to align to True South, not Magnetic South (though in Portugal, the magnetic declination is relatively small, roughly 1º to 3º West depending on whether you are in Faro, Lisbon, or Porto).

## 2. Tilt Angle (Inclination)

The ideal tilt angle depends on whether you can manually adjust the panel or if you plan to fix it permanently into position.

- Year-Round Fixed Position (Recommended for remote nodes): 35º to 38º from the horizontal. 
  - Setting the tilt equal to or slightly below your local latitude (e.g., ~38º in Lisbon) gives you the best total energy yield over a full 12-month period.
- Optimized for Winter Survival: 50º to 55º.
  - If your LoRa bridge runs 24/7, winter is your bottleneck. The sun sits much lower on the horizon, days are shorter, and cloud cover is frequent. Tilting the panel steeper acts like a "catchment net" for that low winter sun and helps rain slide off easily, keeping the panel clean.
- Optimized for Summer Peak: 20º to 25º.
  - The sun is directly overhead, but you likely won't need this adjustment since summer yields plenty of power anyway.
